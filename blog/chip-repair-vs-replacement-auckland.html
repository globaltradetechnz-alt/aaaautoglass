Windscreen Chip Repair vs Replacement Auckland

Not sure if your windscreen needs repair or replacement? Size, location, cost, and insurance — everything Auckland drivers need to know.

Quick Answer

  • Repair is possible if the chip is under 50mm and not in the driver's direct vision
  • Replacement is needed for large cracks, edge damage, or chips in the critical vision area
  • Repair cost (private): $80-$150. Often free under insurance with no excess.
  • Replacement cost (private): $650-$850 for standard vehicles; $800-$1,500+ for ADAS/European.
  • Repair takes 30 minutes; replacement takes 2+ hours
  • We give honest advice — if repair is possible, we will tell you

When Can a Windscreen Chip Be Repaired?

A windscreen chip can often be repaired with resin injection if it meets all of these criteria:

  • Size: Under 50mm in diameter (smaller than a $2 coin)
  • Location: Not in the driver's direct line of sight (the critical vision area in the centre of the windscreen)
  • Depth: Only the outer layer of laminated glass is damaged
  • Type: Bullseye, star, or small crack — not a long spreading crack

If your chip meets these criteria, resin injection is a fast, cost-effective solution that restores the glass's structural integrity and stops the crack from spreading.

When Does a Windscreen Need Replacement?

Replacement is the only safe option if:

  • Size: The damage is larger than 50mm or has spread into a long crack
  • Location: The chip or crack is in the driver's critical vision area
  • Edge damage: The crack extends to the edge of the windscreen
  • Multiple cracks: There are three or more cracks from a single impact
  • Depth: Both layers of the laminated glass are damaged
  • Contamination: The chip is filled with dirt or water that cannot be cleaned out

Insurance: Repair vs Replacement

Most comprehensive insurance policies in NZ cover both chip repair and windscreen replacement. The key difference:

  • Chip repair: Often covered with no excess and no impact on your no-claims bonus
  • Replacement: Usually covered with nil excess (you pay nothing) or just your windscreen excess ($100-$500)
